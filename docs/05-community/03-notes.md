
## Implementation Commitments

To realize the above values and principles, we commit to creating a system that provides:

### Technical Infrastructure

- **Modular architecture** enabling easy addition of new problems and algorithms
- **Standardized interfaces** for problems, algorithms, and performance measures
- **Automatic logging** of all experimental aspects
- **Efficient computational resource management**
- **Distributed execution** of experiments on different platforms
- **Checkpoint and restart** for long-running experiments

### Analytical Tools

- **Exploratory data analysis** with rich visualization sets
- **Automatic statistical tests** with appropriate corrections
- **Effect size analysis** and practical significance
- **Multi-criteria comparisons** of algorithms
- **Sensitivity analysis** to parameters and problem characteristics
- **Performance profiling** over time and problem space

### Repositories and Data

- **Central repository** of benchmark problems with full characterization
- **Algorithm library** from different families and paradigms
- **Results database** from previous experiments for comparisons
- **Metadata** about problems, algorithms, and experiments
- **Versioning system** for all artifacts
- **API** for programmatic access to resources

### Documentation and Education

- **Complete documentation** of architecture and system usage
- **Tutorials** for different usage scenarios
- **Examples** of well-designed benchmarking studies
- **Guides** for result interpretation
- **References** to literature and best practices
- **FAQ** about common problems and questions

### Community and Development

- **Open development process** with public discussions and decisions
- **Community contribution mechanisms** (pull requests, issues)
- **Review process** for new problems and algorithms
- **Regular updates** of benchmark sets
- **Conferences and workshops** promoting best practices
- **Collaboration** with other projects and initiatives

---

## Anti-patterns (what we avoid)

The benchmarking system is NOT:

1. **Algorithm ranking** - we do not create "best to worst" lists
2. **Static collection** - the system evolves with field development
3. **Competition** - the goal is understanding, not winning
4. **Black box** - all processes are transparent and understandable
5. **Isolated project** - we integrate with the benchmarking ecosystem
6. **Marketing tool** - we serve science, not promotion
7. **Substitute for thinking** - results require interpretation and context understanding
